This is a summary of the findings and connections made by the SAMHSA's National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH)

Survey Sample Size and Age of Participating Individuals:

For this survey, a sample size of radomly chosen 70,000 people age 12 and over was used.(NSDUH data)

Data Set Findings and Statistics:

The results found many interesting statistics, and the data statistic that jumped out at me,
but unsuprisingly, was that the highest percentages of substance use, abuse, and dependence
were in the 18-25 age group. I believe this showcases the prominent drug and alchohol abuse problems facing America's youth.
According to the survey, "about two-thirds (66.6%) of people aged 12 or older reported in 2014 that they drank alcohol in the past 12 months, with 6.4% meeting criteria for an alcohol use disorder." (SAMHSA's website)
Along with these statistics, men were accountable for higher rates of drug dependence, Native Americans and Alaskan natives have the highest rates among the various race groups,
and, according to the SAMHSA, "More than 50% of people aged 12 or older in 2011-2012 who used pain relievers for non-medical reasons in the past year got them from a friend or relative."
